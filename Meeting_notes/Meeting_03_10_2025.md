Notes taken from **Section VI (Implementing the Model)** of Heckman
paper:

- Use Bayesian Markov chain Monte Carlo methods to compute sample
  likelihood

- Modelled as outcome variables:

  - Log hourly wages at age 30
  - Employment
  - Occupational choice
  - Educational choice
  - Behavioural outcomes
  - Work experience
  - Fertility choice

- Multinomial probit model used for educational choice model

  - Use schooling levels which are represented by simulations of
    schooling at age 30

- Wage equations at age 30 estimated for individuals at each final
  schooling level

- Dummies included:

  - Race
  - Cohort (Age/Year of birth)
  - Local labour market conditions (check Cameron and Heckman (2001) for
    list of variables used)
  - Region of residence

- Distributions of the unobservables identified nonparametrically

- Last figures in the paper are generated from data from simulations of
  estimates from models and sample data
