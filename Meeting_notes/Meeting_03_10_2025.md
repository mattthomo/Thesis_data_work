# Structural modelling of Heckman (2006)

[Here is the web appendix to the original Heckman
paper](https://jenni.uchicago.edu/noncog/web_supplement.pdf)

Notes taken from **Section VI (Implementing the Model)** of Heckman
paper:

- Use Bayesian Markov chain Monte Carlo methods to compute sample
  likelihood
- “analysis in Carneiro (2003), Hansen (2004) and Heckman and
  Navarro (2006) establishes conditions on the support of the regressors
  that allow for semiparametric identification of the model”
- “for the support conditions for semiparametric identification to hold,
  the support of the distribution for each probability should be the
  unit interval \[0,1\]”

- Wage equations at age 30 estimated for individuals at each final
  schooling level
- Dummies included:
  - Race
  - Cohort (Age/Year of birth)
  - Local labour market conditions (check Cameron and Heckman (2001) for
    list of variables used)
  - Region of residence
- Multivariate probit model used
